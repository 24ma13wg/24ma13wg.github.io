Towards a company skills matrix

September, 2018

A skills matrix lists people along one axis, and skills along another. At the intersections are indicators as to whether or not a person possesses the skill, and if they do, perhaps, to what degree.

It is important for our company to have a skills matrix, so that we know:

  • what skills we are short of – this enables us to recruit new people who have those skills, or provide training opportunities to our own staff;
  • who we have who is able to coach others a particular skill;
  • who has a skill at a project delivery level – this enables us to form the best teams for client projects;
  • who wants to increase a certain skill – this enables us to pair these staff with more skilled staff on project teams; and
  • who is currently learning a skill – this enables our staff to support each other and share knowledge.
